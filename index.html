AI Is Simultaneously Aiding and Replacing Workers: New Federal Reserve Research on Why Experience Premiums Are Rising

✉ Send

A counterintuitive finding: while AI is crushing entry-level hiring in exposed fields, wages are actually rising for experienced workers in those same sectors. The research suggests AI can replicate codified knowledge but not tacit knowledge — meaning it substitutes for junior workers while complementing veterans. Explains a lot about 2026's weird labor market.

Economics Yale Department of Economics

Yale's Pascual Restrepo on the Task-Based Model of AI Automation: Why Job Destruction Depends on New Work Creation

✉ Send

Restrepo, co-author of the most influential economic model of automation with Nobel laureate Daron Acemoglu, explains why AI's effects depend on a race between machines replacing workers and the creation of entirely new tasks.
